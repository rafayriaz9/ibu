Why We Are The story of IBU is the story of all
of us educators.

We have been searching for ways to disrupt education with innovation, but the machine is heavy and unflinching. Our recent experience with a global pandemic has exposed the limitations of an approach to education that was created for another time, another world.

IBU is a Humanist educational vision made reality. We view distance learning as a call to autonomy, mastery, and purpose. This is why we center our design around the learner and their place within an educational community. We are devoted to designing educational platforms for the future visionaries of our planet.

Mentorship

We educators are feeling like novices these days as the imposed physical distance is changing our setting. Being physically separated, we may not be available to guide learners when they need it most. By streamlining content, grading, and admin work, we create opportunities for much deeper mentorship through communication and collaboration tools. In this, we empower students to take an active role in their learning, and relationships.

Social

Social interaction is imperative in education. A socially supportive learning environment is crucial in defining the collaborative learning process. Indeed, we view distance learning as a way to enhance social interactions. Social pressure in a remote environment is less than may be experienced in person. Your LMS can provide a richer and more genuine interaction among learners and educators.

Our LMS is designed to support executive function skills and collaborative group learning.

Technology

Technology inequality is pervasive worldwide. Mobile platforms must be simple & agile, and mobile devices need to be readily available & accessible. Digital Equity is necessary for civic and cultural participation, closing the income gap, closing the opportunity gap, and gaining access to essential services.

We pride ourselves in a robust and agile mobile platform. One that helps dissolve the technology barrier to learning.

Wellbeing

Overcoming excessive screen time and computer vision syndrome seems like a task that is antithetical to remote learning. On the contrary. Distance learning does not necessarily mean more screen time.

Rather than bringing the world to the classroom, the world BECOMES the classroom.

Your LMS can be designed to bring the life of the student to device, rather than the device to the life. It is an important distinction guiding the design of your LMS.
